Get to know about how to give person a first aid in different situations

What is a first aid?

It is the first and immediate help given to a person if he/she is injured. It can prevent further serious injuries or also save a persons life!

Materials required in first aid box

Gloves/Eye protection, CPR pocket mask, tourniquet, roller gauze, 4×4 gauze pads, medical tape, two triangular bandages, sam splint, elastic bandage, trauma shears

First aid for different kinds of injuries:

Cuts and wounds

  • Wash the wound with soap and water to remove germs.
  • Then clean it with cotton soaked in an antiseptic lotion.
  • In case of bleeding, placce some ice cubes on the wound. Then take some cotton wool, press it over the wound and tie it with a bandage.
  • If the bleeding continues, the victim must be taken to a nearby hospital immediately.
  • Consult a doctor for an anti-tetanus injection.

Burns

  • Keep the burnt area under running cold water for a few minutes.
    • OR
  • Put one teaspoon of baking soda in a glass of water.
  • Dip cotton wool in this solution and apply. This will give relief from the burning sensation.
    • OR
  • Apply an anti-burn cream on the affected area.
  • If burns form blisters, never prick them as it may catch infection. If they do break, wash the area with soap and cover them with a clean bandage.
  • Incase of severe burns, take the victim to a nearby hospital immediately.

Insect bites

  • Apply ice or pour cold water on the affected area.
  • Apply calamine lotion to prevent itching in the area.
  • Call a doctor if the victim feels dizzy or have difficulty in breathing.

Snake bites

  • Have the person lie down with wound below the heart. Keep the person calm and at rest, remaining as still as possible to keep venom from spreading.
  • Cover the wound with loose, sterile bandage.
  • Remove any jewelry from the area that was bitten.
  • Immediately consult a doctor.

Splinter

  • Use soap and water to wash the area around the splinter.
  • Clean a pair of tweezers with rubbing alcohol and slowly pull the splinter out.
  • Wash your skin again.
  • If you come across a fragment that is hard to remove, leave it for a day or so to see if it will come out on its own.

Fainting

  • If a person faints, never crowd around him/her. Allow him/her to get fresh air.
  • Loosen his clothes and make him lie down with his legs raised.
  • keep his head lower then the body so that the blood flows faster into the brain.
  • Sprinkle cold water on the victim's faxt to help further regain consciousness and give them something warm to drink.

Nosebleed

  • Sit upright and don't tilt the victim's head back.
  • Loosen any tight clothing around the victim's neck.
  • Pinch the lower end of the nose close to the nostrils and lean forward while the victim applies constant pressure for five to ten minutes. Don't release and check the nose(it could prolong the bleeding).
  • If the nosebleed is the result of trauma, the victim can reduce swelling by holding an ice pack against the bridge of the nose after the bleeding slows down.
  • If it continues for more than 10 minutes or returns later, then immediately take the victim to a doctor.

Sprains and strains

  • Apply an icepack on the strained/sprained area.
  • Do not apply any kind of pressure on the affected area.
  • Try to avoid heat on the affected area.
  • Wrap a bandage around the strained/sprained area to support it.
  • Keep it raised on a pillow as much as possible.

Fracture

  • If the fractured area is bleeding, elevate and apply pressure to the wound using a sterile bandage, a clean cloth, or a clean piece of clothing.
  • If you suspect they’ve broken a bone in their neck or back, help them stay as still as possible. If you suspect they’ve broken a bone in one of their limbs, immobilize the area using a splint or sling.
  • Wrap an ice pack or bag of ice cubes in a piece of cloth and apply it to the injured area for up to 10 minutes at a time.
  • After giving the follwing first aid, you should let the doctor handle the situation.

Drowning

  • Check if the person is breathing.
  • If the person is not breathing, check his/her pulse.
  • If there is no pulse, start CPR.

Poisoning

  • Make sure the victim drinks lot of water or fluids to keep his/her body hydrated.
  • Gradually starting to eat again.
  • If the vomit continues for 12 hours, immediately consult a doctor.
